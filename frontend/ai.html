How the AI Works

SHIELD IQ uses state-of-the-art Natural Language Processing (NLP) and optical intelligence models to evaluate messages for fraud, social engineering, and security threats.

01

Semantic Risk Analysis

Our algorithm breaks down the input text to search for common psychological cues used by bad actors. This includes detecting **high-urgency tone patterns**, pressure tactics, unusual payment requests, and impersonation flags (such as pretending to represent banks, delivery agencies, or support agents).

We analyze grammatical cues, syntactic irregularities, and link/domain properties to assess the validity of the sender's claim.

02

Screenshot OCR Processing

When you upload an image or screenshot, the system utilizes high-accuracy **Optical Character Recognition (OCR)** to transcribe textual content from the image. The extracted text is then passed to our semantic classifier to detect scams within conversations, SMS windows, and emails.

Ephemeral Scan Handling: Both text and image inputs are parsed in memory in real-time. No raw data, images, or chat contents are ever stored on our databases after the scan is finalized.

03

Risk Scoring Engine

The final output is represented as a score from 0 to 100, indicating the severity of the threat:

  • LOW Risk (0-39): Clear message structure, safe or verified domain matching, and normal conversational patterns.
  • MEDIUM Risk (40-69): Suspicious link shorteners, slightly urgent tone, or ambiguous payment contexts requiring vigilance.
  • HIGH Risk (70-100): Extreme urgency, spoofed company signatures, coercive demands for personal details, or known phishing URLs.
